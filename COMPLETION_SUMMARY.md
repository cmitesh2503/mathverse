## How It Works (Simple Explanation)

### The Process
```
AI generates solution with math symbols and diagram
         ↓
Formatter converts to proper display symbols (√, π, ×, etc.)
         ↓
Board display shows: Problem → Figure → Steps → Answer
         ↓
Speech converter: √ becomes "square root", π becomes "pi", etc.
         ↓
Student sees: Clear board with figure and proper symbols
Student hears: Natural speech with mathematical terms
```

## Example Output

### Circle Problem (Chapter 10)

**What the Student Sees** (on board):
```
Problem statement: A point Q is at a distance of 25 cm 
from the center O of a circle, and the length of the 
tangent PQ is 24 cm. Find the radius OP.

Figure/Diagram: 
Draw a circle with center O. Mark point Q outside at 
distance 25 cm. Draw tangent from Q touching at P. 
Mark right angle at P. Show: OQ = 25 cm, PQ = 24 cm, OP = ?

Solution:
Step 1: Identify given values: OQ = 25 cm, PQ = 24 cm
Step 2: The tangent is perpendicular to radius at P, so ∠OPQ = 90°
Step 3: Apply Pythagorean theorem: OP² + PQ² = OQ²
Step 4: Substitute: OP² + 24² = 25²
Step 5: Calculate: OP² + 576 = 625 → OP² = 49
Step 6: Solve: OP = √49 = 7 cm

Final answer: Radius = 7 cm
```

**What the Student Hears**:
> "Now we solve Problem 1. A point Q is at 25 cm from center O. 
> Tangent PQ is 24 cm. Find radius OP. Look at the figure. 
> Step 1: Identify given. OQ equals 25 cm, PQ equals 24 cm.
> Step 2: The tangent is perpendicular to radius at P, so angle OPQ equals 90 degrees.
> Step 3: Apply Pythagorean theorem. OP squared plus PQ squared equals OQ squared.
> ... [continues naturally]"
