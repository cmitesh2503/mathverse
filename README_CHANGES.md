```
Chapter no: 10

Problem statement: 
A point Q is at a distance of 25 cm from the center O 
of a circle, and the length of the tangent PQ is 24 cm. 
Find the radius OP.

Figure/Diagram: 
Draw a circle with center O. Mark point Q outside the 
circle at a distance of 25 cm from O. Draw a tangent 
from Q to the circle, touching at point P. Mark the 
right angle at P. Label: OQ = 25 cm, PQ = 24 cm, OP = ?

Solution:
Source: NCERT Chapter Exercise

Step 1: Identify the given values and what to find.
Given: OQ = 25 cm (center to external point)
Given: PQ = 24 cm (tangent length)  
Find: OP (radius)

Step 2: Choose the appropriate theorem.
The tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius 
at the point of contact (Theorem 10.1).
Therefore: ∠OPQ = 90°

Step 3: Form the right-angled triangle.
Triangle OPQ is right-angled at P.

Step 4: Apply Pythagorean theorem.
OP² + PQ² = OQ²

Step 5: Substitute the known values.
OP² + 24² = 25²
OP² + 576 = 625

Step 6: Solve for OP.
OP² = 625 - 576 = 49
OP = √49 = 7 cm

Final answer: The radius OP of the circle is 7 cm.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════
```
